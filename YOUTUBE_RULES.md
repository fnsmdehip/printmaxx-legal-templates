## Community Guidelines

### Spam and Deceptive Practices

**Not Allowed:**
- Fake engagement (buying views/subscribers)
- Misleading metadata (titles, thumbnails, tags)
- Scams and fraud
- Impersonation
- Link spam
- Artificially inflating metrics
- Selling subscriber accounts

### Sensitive Content

**Not Allowed:**
- Child safety violations (zero tolerance)
- Nudity and sexual content (most cases)
- Suicide and self-harm promotion
- Dangerous challenges
- Eating disorders content

**Restricted (Age-Gated):**
- Mature themes
- Violence in certain contexts
- Drug use depiction
- Vulgar language

### Violent or Dangerous Content

**Not Allowed:**
- Promoting violence
- Graphic violence
- Content glorifying violent events
- Instructions for harmful activities
- Animal abuse

### Hateful Content

**Not Allowed:**
- Content promoting hatred based on:
  - Race, ethnicity
  - Religion
  - Gender, gender identity
  - Sexual orientation
  - Age
  - Disability
  - Nationality
  - Veteran status
  - Caste
  - Immigration status

### Harassment and Cyberbullying

**Not Allowed:**
- Threats
- Doxxing (revealing personal information)
- Malicious insults
- Sustained harassment
- Inciting others to harass

### Misinformation

**Not Allowed:**
- Medical misinformation (vaccines, treatments)
- Election misinformation
- Dangerous conspiracy theories
- Manipulated media intended to mislead

## YouTube Partner Program (YPP)

### Eligibility Requirements

**Current Threshold (Standard):**
- 1,000 subscribers
- 4,000 public watch hours in past 12 months
  OR 10 million Shorts views in past 90 days
- Follow all YouTube policies
- Have AdSense account
- Based in eligible country

**Lower Threshold (Fan Funding Features):**
- 500 subscribers
- 3 public uploads in 90 days
- 3,000 public watch hours OR 3 million Shorts views

### Monetization Features

| Feature | Requirements |
|---------|--------------|
| Ad revenue | Standard YPP |
| Channel memberships | 500+ subscribers |
| Super Chat/Stickers | Live streaming enabled |
| Super Thanks | Standard YPP |
| Shopping | Eligible creators |
| YouTube Premium revenue | Standard YPP |

## Advertiser-Friendly Content Guidelines

### Why This Matters

Even if you're in YPP, videos must be "advertiser-friendly" to earn full ad revenue. Non-compliant videos get:
- Limited or no ads
- Yellow $ icon (limited monetization)
- Lower revenue

### Full Monetization (Green $)

Content that is appropriate for most advertisers:
- Family-friendly or general audience
- No excessive profanity
- No controversial topics
- No graphic content

### Limited Monetization (Yellow $)

Some ads, but reduced revenue:
- Mild profanity
- Mature themes (handled tastefully)
- Some controversial topics
- Violence in news/documentary context

### No Monetization (Red $)

No ads shown:
- Excessive profanity
- Graphic violence
- Drug use
- Hateful content
- Dangerous acts

### Content Categories and Impact

| Category | Impact on Monetization |
|----------|----------------------|
| Inappropriate language | Heavy profanity in first 30 sec = Limited |
| Violence | Graphic violence = None |
| Adult content | Sexual themes = None to Limited |
| Harmful/dangerous acts | Promoting dangerous activities = None |
| Hateful content | Any hateful content = None |
| Drug-related content | Drug promotion = None |
| Controversial issues | May be limited |
| Sensitive events | Recent tragedies = Limited |

## Paid Promotion and Sponsored Content

### Disclosure Requirements

**YouTube's "Includes Paid Promotion" Checkbox:**
1. Go to video details
2. Check "Video contains paid promotion"
3. Label appears on video

**When Required:**
- Sponsored videos
- Product placements
- Any compensation for featuring products
- Free products received for review

### FTC Compliance (Separate Requirement)

YouTube's label alone may not satisfy FTC requirements. Also include:
- Verbal disclosure in video ("Sponsored by [Brand]")
- Written disclosure in description
- Clear and conspicuous placement

**Disclosure Example:**

Video:
> "Before we start, I want to mention that this video is sponsored by [Brand]. They're paying me to share my honest thoughts about [Product]."

Description:
> "DISCLOSURE: This video is sponsored by [Brand]. I received compensation for this video. All opinions are my own."

### Prohibited Sponsorships

Cannot accept paid promotions for:
- Illegal products
- Counterfeit goods
- Products violating YouTube policies
- Inappropriate products for your audience

## Copyright

### Content ID System

YouTube's automated copyright detection:
- Scans uploads against database
- May result in: claim, block, track
- Affects monetization

### Copyright Strikes

**Three-Strike System:**
1. **First strike:** Warning, features restricted
2. **Second strike:** More restrictions
3. **Third strike:** Channel termination

Strikes expire after 90 days if no further violations.

### Fair Use

**May qualify (but risky):**
- Commentary and criticism
- Educational use
- Parody
- News reporting
- Transformative use

**Important:** Fair use is a legal defense, not a right. Content ID doesn't consider fair use automatically.

### Music Usage

**Options:**
- YouTube Audio Library (free, royalty-free)
- Licensed music (direct deals)
- Creative Commons music
- Original music

**Risk:** Using popular music without license = claims/strikes

## Shorts Policies

### Monetization

- Shorts Fund (legacy) replaced by ad revenue sharing
- Ads between Shorts in feed
- Revenue based on views and engagement

### Content Rules

Same as regular videos, plus:
- No watermarks from other platforms (reduced distribution)
- Original content preferred
- Reposted content may get limited distribution

### Music in Shorts

- Can use licensed music from YouTube library
- Different rules than long-form videos
- Revenue sharing impacts when using licensed music

## Enforcement

### Strike System

| Violation Level | Consequence |
|-----------------|-------------|
| First warning | Education, no strike |
| First strike | Features restricted 1 week |
| Second strike | No uploads for 2 weeks |
| Third strike | Channel terminated |

### Appealing

**Video Removal:**
1. Go to YouTube Studio
2. Content tab
3. Find restricted video
4. Click "Appeal"

**Strike Appeal:**
1. Go to Channel settings
2. Feature eligibility
3. Follow appeal instructions

**Timeline:** Appeals typically reviewed within 5-7 days.

## Best Practices for PRINTMAXX Content

### Advertiser-Friendly Tips

**Do:**
- Keep heavy profanity out of first 30 seconds (and title/thumbnail)
- Use content warnings for mature topics
- Focus on educational value
- Maintain professional tone

**Don't:**
- Make exaggerated income claims
- Use clickbait titles/thumbnails
- Include graphic content
- Use others' content without permission

### Sponsorship Best Practices

1. Use YouTube's paid promotion checkbox
2. Verbally disclose within first 30 seconds
3. Include written disclosure in description
4. Be honest about relationship with brand
5. Follow FTC guidelines

### Thumbnail and Title Guidelines

**Safe:**
- Accurate representation of content
- No excessive text
- No misleading imagery
- No sexually suggestive imagery
- No graphic imagery

**Risky:**
- Clickbait (may get penalized)
- Misleading thumbnails (policy violation)
- Shocking imagery (may trigger restrictions)
