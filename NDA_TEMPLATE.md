## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 "Confidential Information" means:

Any non-public information disclosed by either Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether oral, written, electronic, or visual, including but not limited to:

- Business plans and strategies
- Financial information and projections
- Customer and supplier lists
- Marketing plans and data
- Product specifications and roadmaps
- Technical data and know-how
- Software and source code
- Trade secrets
- Pricing information
- Employee information
- Any information marked "confidential" or "proprietary"

### 1.2 Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party

b) Was lawfully in Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, as evidenced by written records

c) Is lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions

d) Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information

e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order (subject to Section 4)

---

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 2.1 Protection of Confidential Information

The Receiving Party agrees to:

a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence

b) Use Confidential Information only for the purpose of evaluating and pursuing the potential business relationship ("Permitted Purpose")

c) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the Disclosing Party

d) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care

e) Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, and advisors who:
   - Need to know the information for the Permitted Purpose
   - Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement

### 2.2 No Rights Granted

This Agreement does not grant any rights or licenses to the Receiving Party in the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, except the limited right to use for the Permitted Purpose.

### 2.3 No Obligation to Proceed

Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to:

- Enter into any business relationship
- Proceed with any transaction
- Disclose any particular information

---

## 3. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

### 3.1 Upon Request or Termination

Upon written request of the Disclosing Party or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party will:

a) Return all tangible materials containing Confidential Information

b) Delete or destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information

c) Provide written certification of compliance within [10] business days

### 3.2 Exceptions

Receiving Party may retain:

- Copies required by law or regulation
- Archival copies made in the ordinary course of electronic backup (not for active use)

Such retained copies remain subject to this Agreement's confidentiality obligations.

---

## 4. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose Confidential Information:

a) Receiving Party will promptly notify Disclosing Party (to the extent legally permitted)

b) Receiving Party will cooperate with Disclosing Party's efforts to seek a protective order

c) Receiving Party will disclose only the minimum information required

d) Disclosed information retains its confidential status under this Agreement

---

## 5. OWNERSHIP

### 5.1 Retained Ownership

All Confidential Information remains the property of the Disclosing Party. This Agreement does not transfer any ownership rights.

### 5.2 No Warranty

CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS." THE DISCLOSING PARTY MAKES NO WARRANTIES REGARDING ACCURACY, COMPLETENESS, OR FITNESS FOR ANY PURPOSE.

---

### 6.2 Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement with [30] days written notice.

### 6.3 Survival

Confidentiality obligations survive termination for:

**Option A - Fixed Period:**
[3/5] years from the date of disclosure

**Option B - Trade Secrets:**
- Trade secrets: As long as information remains a trade secret
- Other Confidential Information: [3] years from disclosure

## 7. REMEDIES

### 7.1 Irreparable Harm

The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm not adequately compensated by monetary damages.

### 7.2 Equitable Relief

The Disclosing Party is entitled to seek injunctive relief and specific performance, without the need to post bond, in addition to any other remedies available at law.

## 8. NO SOLICITATION (Optional)

During the term and for [12] months after termination, neither Party will directly solicit for employment any employee of the other Party with whom they had contact in connection with the Permitted Purpose.

## ALTERNATIVE: ONE-WAY NDA

If only one party will be disclosing confidential information, use this modified version:

**Remove:** References to "mutual" and "each Party as Disclosing/Receiving Party"

**Replace with:**

"Disclosing Party" = [COMPANY NAME]
"Receiving Party" = [OTHER PARTY NAME]

**Modify Section 2.1(d):** Remove reference to protecting own confidential information (since Receiving Party may not have comparable information).

## NOTES ON USING THIS TEMPLATE

### When to Use an NDA

- Discussing potential partnerships
- Sharing business strategies with contractors
- Exploring acquisition or investment
- Hiring consultants who will access sensitive information
- Any situation where you share non-public business information

### Key Decisions to Make

1. **Mutual vs. One-Way:** Will both parties share confidential information?

2. **Duration:** How long should confidentiality last?
   - Standard: 2-3 years
   - Trade secrets: Until no longer a trade secret

3. **Scope:** What information will be shared?

4. **Permitted Purpose:** What can the recipient do with the information?

### Red Flags in NDAs You Receive

- Unlimited confidentiality duration
- Overly broad definition of confidential information
- Restrictions on hiring employees (anti-competitive)
- Assignment rights you did not agree to
- Jurisdiction in inconvenient location
- No carve-outs for publicly available information
